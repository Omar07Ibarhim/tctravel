Baku City Tour

​Start
  • –
    ​The tour starts from the site in front of the Maiden’s Tower, which is a symbol of the city of Baku and is included in the UNESCO World Heritage List. According to the history and mission of this tower, there are several scientific versions and an infinite number of legends that your professional guide will tell you in more detail.
​Church of St. Bartholomew
  • –
    ​Opposite the Maiden’s Tower, the ruins of the “Church of St. Bartholomew”, which, as you know, was one of the 12 apostles of Jesus Christ and was engaged in the spread of Christianity in Albania and Asia. In 71, he was crucified by fire-worshipers. It was at the place of his death that this church was later built.
​The oldest bath
  • –
    ​The largest and oldest bathhouse that has come down to us in the Old Town is Hamam Haji Baani. Until 1960, no one knew about her, since she was bombarded with earth. On the surface were numerous shacks and houses, the inhabitants of which for hundreds of years did not realize that an ancient monument was buried under their feet.
​Multani caravanserai
  • –
    ​Multani caravanserai was created in the XIV century. Multani Caravanserai was built for Indian merchants - fire worshipers who came from the city of Multan in India. Opposite Multani is the Bukhara caravanserai, which was built at the end of the 15th century on a trade route passing through the Shemakha gate. Basically, merchants and travelers from Central Asia, in particular from Bukhara, stayed in it, from where it got its name. The narrow street between the two caravansaries was part of the Great Silk Road.
​Sailor's house
  • –
    ​This house belonged to a sailor. That is why the construction resembles the deck of a ship. Now there is a carpet workshop. The "House with Chains" before the Soviet regime belonged to the local merchant Haji Mamed Huseyn. Until recently, this building was the office of the oil companies BP and Statoil. Currently, this is the building of the Museum of Archeology.
​House of Baku Khans
  • –

​​The four-cornered tower
  • –
    ​The four-cornered tower (dungeon) of the city walls was built in the 15th century. According to medieval sources, in Baku there were 70 half-towers and one quadrangular tower located in the northern part of the fortress walls. In the Middle Ages, this tower was used as a reinforcement of the defensive ability of the fortress walls from the north, as well as a storage room for weapons.
​Old City
  • –
    ​One of the most important architectural masterpieces of the Old City is the Shirvanshahs Palace, which is also called the pearl of Baku. This tour will include the entrance to the palace, from which you will have unforgettable impressions from the combination of bright walls and dark stories.
​Juma Mosque
  • –
    ​The last attraction on this tour will be the Juma Mosque, located in the vicinity of the Maiden Tower. During its existence, the mosque was repeatedly and radically updated and rebuilt. In 1437, during the reign of Shirvanshah Khalil-Ulla I from the Derbendi dynasty, a minaret was built near the mosque, which has survived to this day.
